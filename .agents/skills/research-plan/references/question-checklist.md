# Question Checklist for Research Planning

Ask only the missing items.

## Universal questions
1. What exact question or decision should the research support?
2. What output do you want at the end?
3. Where should I start?
4. What should I exclude?
5. What would make the result useful enough to stop?

## Useful AI/ML-specific questions
- Should I prioritize papers, official repos, model cards, or benchmarks?
- Do you care more about SOTA performance, reproducibility, implementation quality, or practical usability?
- Are closed-source models allowed in the comparison?
- Are third-party leaderboards acceptable, or should I verify only primary evidence?
- Do you want recent work only, or should older foundational sources stay in scope?

## Good multiple-choice prompts
- Deliverable: memo / literature review / benchmark audit / reproduction plan / shortlist
- Scope: seed-only / seed-plus-adjacent / broad search after approval
- Source policy: primary-only / primary-plus-official commentary / anything traceable
